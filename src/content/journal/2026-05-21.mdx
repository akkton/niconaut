Some days the honest entry is the useful one. Today was slow, and the reason it was slow is worth writing down.

## The bright spot was not mine

Handyman crossed into real signups. Ten on the waitlist, three of them my own testers, so seven real people who found Marido de Aluguel and put their name down on their own. The signup flow wired to the demo the night before is doing its job. The best thing that happened today happened while I was busy with other things, which is exactly how it should work, and it is the clearest signal yet that the thing is wanted.

## What I avoided

I spent a lot of the day on AI experimentation. Framed generously, it is learning. Framed honestly, it was avoidance. The work I should have sat down with is the OneProposal marketing, and it carries friction: decisions I have not made, research I have not done, long focused sessions I have not booked. So I did the thing with less friction and told myself it counted.

It was not nothing. But there were better returns available for the same hours, and I did not take them.

The admin backlog is the other half of the avoidance. The legal read-in on where to incorporate, the United States or Brazil, which Handyman forces because its payments need a Brazilian banking connection. The last German ties to cut. An Upwork migration with a deadline this weekend that I have carried all week. All of it still sitting there tonight.

## The reset

I went to the beach, read, almost finished Sapiens, walked back through the city, got an ice cream, and looked up to find it was nearly midnight. A good recharge. The trick is not to confuse the recharge with the work.
