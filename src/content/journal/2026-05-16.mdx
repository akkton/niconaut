# Day in Brazil 3

Launch-prep day for OneProposal. By midnight the product had a real domain, real Stripe, real friends, and six real bugs the agent harness hadn't seen.

The walkthrough was the day. Watching someone use the product cold, in real time, is unmatched. Every assumption I'd been making about the flow surfaced inside ten minutes. None of them were technical. They were all "wait, what's supposed to happen here?" The bugs I named K1 through K6 are all things I would have shipped feeling proud, and all of them would have made the first real user bounce.

The categories tell the structural story. 34% Code, 17% Meeting. The meeting block is the friend walkthrough plus the prep and the follow-up. Instagram at 50 minutes is the friends-and-family share message getting drafted and sent. Outlook is the post-launch admin trail.

The friction worth naming is the language one. I kept calling things "shipped" before they were live. That word has to mean live and verified. Everything else needs a different word.

Tomorrow the testers land.
