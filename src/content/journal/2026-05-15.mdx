# Day in Brazil 2

The second full day at the new desk. Settled in enough that real work could actually start, and it did. Eight PRs deep into a client n8n migration by end of day: ID-resilient filtering, template residue stripped from the docs, an inline-highlight comment system that mimics Google Docs.

The lesson I took home wasn't about the output, though. It was about almost missing the brief.

The client flagged it midway through the morning. I was building toward "scenarios built" when the actual goal was "ID-resilient + professionally documented." Two different finish lines. The pipeline was humming, but it was humming toward the wrong one. Fast-and-wrong is what you get when nobody is actively holding the primary constraint visible.

WhatsApp at 76 minutes. Family stays in the rhythm. Brazil is still family.
